### Key principles for drafting (follow these)

- **Accounting systems evolved — never say "designed."** Reserve "design choice" for specific features (conventions, identity, decompositions), not for the system as a whole.
- **Never use "measurement" or "measure."** Use express, quantify, estimate, assess, allocate.
- **Never use "the answer" or imply the system produces answers.** It produces representations that inform judgment.
- **Use "worth" in preference to "value"** (except for technical terms like "fair value").
- **Flow statements are decompositions, not independent reports.** Never use "articulation" or "consistent."
- **The identity is imposed, not derived or discovered.**
- **Write A − L = E** (but introduce A = L + E first).
- **Equities is "the difference," not "what's left."**
- **Cash: you just count it.**
- **Dividends are "declared and paid"** — don't separate declaration from payment.
- **We are "helping them learn," not "teaching them."**
- **Write as if explaining in conversation** — warm, intellectually serious, no jargon without purpose.
- **Never present a rule without its tradeoff.**
- **Information framing, not measurement framing.**
