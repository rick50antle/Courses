### Key principles established in earlier objectives (follow these)

- **Don't ask "why" when you want the student to demonstrate a consequence.** "Explain why X" invites philosophical answers. "Given this scenario, show what happens" is assessable.
- **Give two alternatives, ask the student to reason about them.** Works for conventions, recognition rules, entity boundaries.
- **Multiple choice is appropriate** for students at this stage who can reason about alternatives but can't generate examples from scratch.
- **"Which one is right?" is a powerful discriminator.** The student who says "neither" has internalized the insight.
- **Frame positively.** Flow statements are an opportunity to add information, not just "not independent reports." Decomposition tells you HOW the change happened, not just THAT it happened.
- **"Less costly to audit" not "easier to audit."** Precision matters because it points to real economic consequences.
- **Information framing, not measurement framing.** The system is designed to convey information. When the same architecture works for corporations and nonprofits, that's evidence it's about information design, not measurement.
