## What Was Done This Session

### 1. Completed the One-by-One Objective Review (Objectives 14–19)

Picked up where the first session left off and reviewed Objectives 14–19 using the same approach: restate around assessable student behavior, design concrete assessments, present to Rick for discussion.

**Objective 14 — What each flow statement decomposes, and the nesting relationship.** Restated to require students to identify which change each flow statement decomposes, explain what information each decomposition adds, and explain the nesting of the income statement within the statement of changes in equities. Assessment has two parts: (1) matching with justification — not just labeling, but explaining what each decomposition adds; (2) nesting multiple choice — "Net income appears as a single line in the SCE. What does this tell you about the relationship?"

**Objective 15 — Why cash gets its own statement.** Two-legged justification: (1) cash is the convention-free benchmark — you just count it — so comparing CFO to NI reveals how much of the income statement is shaped by conventions; (2) cash is the most liquid and portable asset, making it the biggest target for theft — tracking its movements serves the system's oversight and safeguarding function. Assessment uses a "select the two strongest reasons" format. Rick contributed the theft/safeguarding leg.

**Objective 16 — NI vs. CFO gap: trace it.** Restated to require tracing specific sources of the gap from the eight-transaction entity. Light tracing — the course will carry the operational weight. Multiple choice checks whether the student understands the gap is not an error but two different lenses on the same activity.

**Objective 17 — NI vs. CFO gap: interpret it.** Two entities with the same net income ($50,000) but different CFO ($48,000 vs. $12,000). Interpretive question plus a multiple choice about a persistent three-year pattern. Flagged as a stretch objective because the prework shows only one period.

**Objective 18 — Convention spectrum (lightened).** Rick judged the original version premature — the prework hasn't presented a systematic item-by-item analysis of convention-dependence. Lightened to a single discrimination: which of three items (cash, equipment, receivable) involves the most convention-dependent judgment? Rick added that equipment involves not just depreciation choices but ongoing assessments of whether the asset is still generating expected benefits. Multiple convention choices and predictions accumulate over time.

**Objective 19 — Read statements together, drilling down from retained earnings.** Rick directed this to start from the change in retained earnings and drill down. Three-step structure: (1) What can you determine from the $19,000 change alone? (pick the best answer); (2) What did the income statement add? (3) What does comparing to CFO add? Rick corrected the distractor analysis — if retained earnings increased, the entity was profitable, but you don't know how profitable. Also corrected the dividend language: retained earnings is debited by dividends declared, not paid; in the prework we say "declared and paid" to keep it simple.

### 5. Two New Terminology Conventions

**(a) Accounting systems evolved — don't say "designed."** The system evolved over centuries; it did not emerge from a blueprint. Reserve "design choice" for specific features within the evolved system — conventions, the identity, decomposition categories — not for the system as a whole.

**(b) Dividends are "declared and paid."** In the prework, treat declaration and payment as simultaneous. In practice they're distinct (debit RE on declaration, debit the liability on payment), but separating them would distract from the structural points.

6. **Objective 15 has two legs** — convention-free benchmark (epistemic) and theft target (oversight). Rick contributed the second leg.

7. **Objective 17 is a stretch objective** — the single-period version is supported; the persistent-pattern version goes beyond current content.
